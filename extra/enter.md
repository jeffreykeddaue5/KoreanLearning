들다 = something moves up, into, or enters a new state (physical or abstract).
(Up/in) (Into a mind/state) (Entering a condition) (Resources being consumed)

| Verb / Expression | Meaning                          | Korean Example                 | English Translation                     | Side Note                              |
|------------------|----------------------------------|--------------------------------|-----------------------------------------|----------------------------------------|
| 들다             | to lift / hold                   | 가방을 들었어요.               | I picked up the bag.                    | Core physical meaning                  |
| 들고 있다        | to be holding                    | 컵을 들고 있어요.              | I’m holding a cup.                      | Continuous state                       |
| 들다             | to take / cost                   | 돈이 많이 들어요.              | It costs a lot of money.                | Time / money / effort pattern          |
| 마음에 들다      | to like                          | 이 신발이 마음에 들어요.        | I like these shoes.                     | Fixed expression                      |
| 생각이 들다      | a thought occurs                 | 그런 생각이 들었어요.          | That thought crossed my mind.           | Very common                            |
| 잠이 들다        | to fall asleep                   | 바로 잠이 들었어요.            | I fell asleep right away.               | Change of state                        |
| 겁이 들다        | to become afraid                 | 갑자기 겁이 들었어요.          | I suddenly felt scared.                 | Emotion enters                         |
| 철이 들다        | to mature                        | 이제 철이 들었네.              | You’ve matured now.                     | Idiomatic                             |
| 손이 많이 들다   | to take a lot of effort          | 이 일은 손이 많이 들어요.      | This work takes a lot of effort.        | Idiom (not literal hands)              |
| 사람이 들다      | to fit (capacity)                | 몇 명이나 들어요?              | How many people fit?                    | Space / container use                  |
| 집에 들다        | to enter (formal / written)      | 함부로 들지 마세요.            | Do not enter without permission.        | Sounds formal or literary              |
| 들어가다         | to go in                         | 방에 들어갔어요.               | I went into the room.                   | 들다 + 가다                           |
| 들어오다         | to come in                       | 안으로 들어오세요.             | Please come inside.                     | Toward speaker                        |
| 들어보다         | to listen to / try listening     | 그 노래 들어봤어요?            | Have you listened to that song?         | Experience-based                      |
| 들어주다         | to listen / grant                | 내 말 좀 들어줘.               | Please listen to me.                    | Often emotional                        |
| 들어올리다       | to lift up                       | 상자를 들어올렸어요.           | I lifted the box up.                    | Emphasizes upward motion               |
| 들여다보다       | to look into                     | 책을 들여다봤어요.             | I looked into the book.                 | Fixed compound verb                    |
| 들여놓다         | to bring in                      | 짐을 안으로 들여놓았어요.      | I brought the luggage inside.           | Directional                            |
| 들이마시다       | to inhale                        | 숨을 깊게 들이마셔요.          | Take a deep breath in.                  | Opposite: 내쉬다                      |
| 들이키다         | to gulp / drink fast             | 물을 한 번에 들이켰어요.       | I gulped down the water.                | Casual / vivid                        |
| 들이다           | to invest / put in               | 시간과 노력을 들였어요.        | I invested time and effort.             | Abstract use                           |
| 들이대다         | to shove / push toward           | 카메라를 들이댔어요.           | He shoved the camera at me.             | Often negative nuance                  |
